Medicinal Chemistry and Drug Design

Modern medicines are not discovered by chance alone. They are carefully designed using the principles of :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}.

Medicinal chemists modify molecules to improve their safety, effectiveness, and stability. Small changes in chemical structure can greatly affect how a drug interacts with the body.

For example, insulin analogues such as :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7} were created by modifying the amino-acid sequence of natural insulin. These modifications allow the drug to act longer in the body.

Medicinal chemistry bridges chemistry, biology, and pharmacology to create life-saving drugs.
