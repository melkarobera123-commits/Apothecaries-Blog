The Discovery of Penicillin

The antibiotic era began with the discovery of :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}.

In 1928, :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4} noticed that a mold had contaminated one of his petri dishes. Surprisingly, bacteria around the mold had died.

The mold, :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}, produced a substance that killed bacteria. This compound was later developed into penicillin.

During World War II, penicillin saved millions of lives by treating bacterial infections that were once fatal.
