The Story of Aspirin

How willow bark became the world's most famous medicine

Long before aspirin existed, people used willow bark for pain and fever relief.

In 1897, chemist Felix Hoffmann at Bayer synthesized acetylsalicylic acid, which became aspirin.

💡 Fun Fact: Aspirin was originally called “Herbalin” in early trials!
